Canele

Bordeaux, France ~ This recipe has its origin in the south of France.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Cups whole milk
  • 1 vanilla bean pod
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 1/4 cups of sugar
  • 1 cup flour
  • 4 Tbsps unsalted butter
  • 1 Tbsp light rum
  • Confectioners sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Pour milk into saucepan. Scrape the vanilla seeds into the milk and add the pod. Warm the milk over medium high heat until small bubbles form along the edges of the pan, about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat and set aside to cool.
  2. In a small bowl, beat the eggs. In another bowl, beat the egg yolks.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar and the flour. Make a well in the center and whisk in the whole eggs followed by the egg yolks to make a thick paste. Remove the vanilla bean pod from the milk and discard. Add the warm milk, melted butter, and rum to the egg mixture and whick until blended. Cover the batter and refrigerate for at lest 1 hour or up to 2 hours.
  4. Preheat the oven to 350F. Coat canele mold molds with some of the butter and refrigerate for for 15 minutes.
  5. Remove the batter from the refrigerator and stir well. Fill each chilled mold almost to the rim. Bake until the caneles are dark brown and puffed on the edges, with a slight depression in the center, about 1 hour; do not open the oven during baking. Unmold the caneles onto a wire while still hot.
  6. Continue buttering, chilling and filling the molds, and baking the caneles until all batter has been used.
  7. Serve at room temperature.

These are caneles that were spotted in a farmers market in Montreal.
